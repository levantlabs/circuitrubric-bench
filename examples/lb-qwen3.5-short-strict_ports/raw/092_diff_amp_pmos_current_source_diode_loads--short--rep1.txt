* PMOS Differential Amplifier with Parallel Current-Source and Diode-Connected Loads
* Topology: Two PMOS input transistors (M1, M2) driven by a tail current source (MTail).
* Load: Two parallel branches per side consisting of a PMOS active load (current source) 
*       and a diode-connected PMOS device. Note: Per standard IC design polarity conventions 
*       for an NMOS amplifier, one would use NMOS loads. However, the prompt requests a 
*       "PMOS differential amplifier". To maintain valid DC biasing where the input devices 
*       are PMOS (sourcing current from VDD), the load devices must sink current to ground.
*       Therefore, the active loads and diode-connected devices must be NMOS.
*       This creates a standard CMOS differential pair topology.
