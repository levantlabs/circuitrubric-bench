* Cascoded NMOS Common-Source Amplifier with Cascoded PMOS Current-Source Load
* Topology: 
*   Input Stage: NMOS (M1) driven by external gate voltage.
*   Active Load: PMOS Current Mirror stack (M2 cascoding M3).
*   Output Node: Drain of M4 (cascode device) connected to Drain of M2 (cascode device).

* NMOS Amplifier Devices
M1 out in s1 nmos W=10u L=1u
M2 out c1 s1 pmos W=10u L=1u
M3 c1 vdd s1 pmos W=10u L=1u

* PMOS Current Source Load Devices (Cascoded Stack)
* M4 is the cascode device for the load current source.
* M5 is the main current source transistor.
* M6 and M7 form a simple diode-connected bias generator to set Vbias for M4/M5 stack.
* Note: In a pure gain block without a specific bias network request, we define the devices 
* that create the topology. The bulk terminals are tied to their respective supplies.

M4 c1 out s2 pmos W=10u L=1u
M5 s2 vdd s2 pmos W=10u L=1u
M6 bias_gen_1 vdd vdd nmos W=1u L=1u
M7 bias_gen_2 vdd vdd pmos W=1u L=1u

* Device Models (Standard Parameters)
.model nmos nmos (level=1 VTO=0.4 KP=110u CGDO=0 CGSO=0)
.model pmos pmos (level=1 VTO=-0.4 KP=50u CGDO=0 CGSO=0)
