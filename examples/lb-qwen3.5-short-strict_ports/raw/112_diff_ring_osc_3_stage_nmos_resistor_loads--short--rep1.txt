1.  **Topology Conflict**: A differential amplifier stage with resistive loads (active or passive) is a linear gain block. It does not inherently contain the positive feedback required for oscillation.
2.  **Ring Oscillator Definition**: A ring oscillator consists of an *odd* number of inverting stages connected in a loop.
3.  **Inversion Requirement**: To form a ring oscillator, each stage must be an **inverting** amplifier (e.g., Common Source).
4.  **Differential vs. Inverting**: A standard differential pair with resistive loads is non-inverting at the output of one side relative to the input of that same side (if looking at single-ended output from a differential input, it's half-circuit behavior). 5.  **Resistor Loads**: While NMOS common-source stages *can* use resistor loads to provide gain and inversion, they are rarely used in integrated IC ring oscillators (which prefer current-source loads for higher $f_T$). However, the prompt explicitly requests "resistor loads".
